Purpose

This document describes the integration of risk management into the organization according to ISO 31000 Clause 5.4. It defines how risk management is embedded in all organizational activities.

Scope

This document applies to: - AdminSend GmbH - All organizational levels and functions - Business processes and projects - Decision-making processes

Integration Principle

Risk management is most effective when it is an integral part of all organizational activities, including decision-making, planning, governance, and reporting.

Integration Objectives

  1. Embedding in Governance
  2. Part of organizational structure
  3. Integrated into leadership processes

  4. Included in reporting

  5. Embedding in Strategy

  6. Risks in strategy development
  7. Risk-based goal setting

  8. Strategic risk planning

  9. Embedding in Processes

  10. Risk management in all processes
  11. Standardized approaches
  12. Continuous risk consideration

Integration Areas

1. Organizational Structure

Governance Integration: - Risk management in organizational structure - Clear responsibilities and authorities - Risk management committees and bodies - Reporting lines and escalation paths

Roles and Responsibilities:

Level Integration
Board Risk oversight and strategic direction
Executive Management Risk management implementation
Department Heads Risk management in departments
Employees Risk awareness in daily operations

2. Leadership and Management Processes

Strategic Planning: - Risk analysis in strategy development - Risk scenarios in planning - Risk-based resource allocation

Operational Planning: - Risks in annual planning - Risk management in budgeting - Risk assessment in project planning

Performance Management: - Risk KPIs in Balanced Scorecard - Risk management in objectives - Risk-based performance evaluation

3. Decision-Making

Decision Processes: - Risk assessment before decisions - Risk-based decision criteria - Documentation of risk considerations

Decision Levels:

Decision Type Risk Management Integration
Strategic Comprehensive risk analysis required
Tactical Risk assessment in planning
Operational Risk checklists and criteria

4. Business Processes

Core Processes: - Risk management in process documentation - Risk controls in process steps - Risk indicators in process monitoring

Support Processes: - HR: Risk awareness in training - IT: Risk management in IT governance - Finance: Risks in financial planning - Compliance: Risks in compliance management

5. Project Management

Project Phases: - Initiation: Risk assessment in business case - Planning: Create risk management plan - Execution: Monitor and control risks - Closure: Document lessons learned

Project Risk Management: - Risk identification in project start - Risk assessment in project planning - Risk control during project execution - Risk reporting to stakeholders

Integration Mechanisms

1. Policies and Procedures

  • Risk management in corporate policies
  • Risk assessment in standard procedures
  • Risk criteria in work instructions

2. Systems and Tools

  • Risk management software
  • Integration into ERP systems
  • Risk dashboards and reports
  • Automated risk monitoring

3. Training and Awareness

  • Risk management in onboarding
  • Regular risk management training
  • Risk awareness in corporate culture
  • Communication of risk information

4. Communication and Reporting

  • Risks in management reports
  • Risk dashboards for executives
  • Risk communication to stakeholders
  • Transparency about risk situation

Integration Levels

Level 1: Basic Integration

  • Risk management as separate function
  • Basic risk identification
  • Reactive risk treatment

Level 2: Functional Integration

  • Risk management in key processes
  • Systematic risk assessment
  • Proactive risk control

Level 3: Full Integration

  • Risk management in all activities
  • Risk awareness in corporate culture
  • Continuous risk management improvement

Success Factors

  1. Top Management Support: Visible commitment
  2. Clear Responsibilities: Defined roles
  3. Adequate Resources: Personnel and systems
  4. Effective Communication: Transparency and dialogue
  5. Continual Improvement: Learning and adaptation
