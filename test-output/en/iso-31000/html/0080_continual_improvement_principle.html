Purpose

This document describes the "Continual improvement" principle of ISO 31000 risk management. Risk management is continually improved through learning and experience.

Principle Description

Risk management is continually improved through learning and experience. Organizations should develop and implement strategies to improve their risk management maturity along with all other aspects of their organization.

Key Statements

  • Risk management is a continuous process
  • Learning from experiences is central
  • Maturity development is pursued
  • Adaptation to new insights
  • Systematic improvement

PDCA Cycle in Risk Management:

  1. Plan

Maturity Model

Risk Management Maturity Levels

Level 1 - Initial: - Ad-hoc risk management - No standardized processes - Reactive approach

Level 2 - Repeatable: - Basic processes established - Documented procedures - Repeatable practices

Level 3 - Defined: - Standardized processes - Organization-wide application - Integrated risk management

Level 4 - Managed: - Quantitative control - Measurable objectives - Proactive management

Level 5 - Optimizing: - Continuous optimization - Innovation and improvement - Anticipatory management

Improvement Measures

Identification of Improvement Potentials

Sources for Improvements: - Internal audits - External assessments - Stakeholder feedback - Incident analyses - Industry developments - Regulatory changes

Prioritization of Improvements

Criteria: - Impact on risk management effectiveness - Resource requirements - Urgency - Strategic significance - Cost-benefit ratio

Measuring Improvement

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Risk management maturity level
  • Number of identified risks
  • Effectiveness of risk mitigation measures
  • Time spent on risk processes
  • Stakeholder satisfaction
  • Number of realized risks
  • Cost of risk events

Success Measurement

Annual Assessment: - Review of risk management framework - Analysis of objective achievement - Assessment of process efficiency - Identification of improvement potentials - Planning for next year
