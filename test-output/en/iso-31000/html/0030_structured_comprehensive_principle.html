Purpose

This document describes the "Structured and comprehensive" principle of ISO 31000 risk management. A structured and comprehensive approach contributes to consistent and comparable results.

Principle Description

A systematic, timely, and structured approach to risk management contributes to efficiency and to consistent, comparable, and reliable results.

Key Statements

  • Risk management follows a defined process
  • All relevant risks are considered
  • Methods and tools are standardized
  • Results are comparable and traceable

Implementation in AdminSend GmbH

Risk Management Process

  1. Context establishment: Definition of framework conditions
  2. Risk identification: Systematic capture of risks
  3. Risk analysis: Assessment of likelihood and impact
  4. Risk evaluation: Prioritization by significance
  5. Risk treatment: Development of measures
  6. Monitoring: Continuous control

Standardized Methods

Method Application Area
Risk matrix Assessment and prioritization
SWOT analysis Strategic risks
Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) Process and product risks
Scenario analysis Complex risk situations
Bow-tie analysis Cause-effect relationships
