Leadership and Commitment

Purpose

This document describes the requirements for leadership and commitment in risk management according to ISO 31000 Clause 5.3. It defines top management responsibilities for effective risk management.

Scope

This document applies to: - AdminSend GmbH - Board and executive management - Leaders at all levels - Risk management officers

Leadership Responsibility

Top management bears overall responsibility for risk management and must ensure that risk management is integrated into all organizational activities.

Top Management Commitments

  1. Take Responsibility
  2. Accountability for risk management
  3. Demonstrate leadership role

  4. Promote risk management culture

  5. Establish Policy and Objectives

  6. Develop risk management policy
  7. Define strategic risk objectives

  8. Establish risk appetite

  9. Provide Resources

  10. Adequate personnel resources
  11. Ensure financial means

  12. Provide technical infrastructure

  13. Ensure Integration

  14. Embed risk management in strategy
  15. Integrate into business processes
  16. Include in decision-making

Risk Management Policy

Policy Elements

The risk management policy must contain:

Element Description
Purpose Why risk management is conducted
Scope Where risk management is applied
Commitment Top management engagement
Responsibilities Who is responsible for what
Resources What means are provided
Review How the policy is reviewed

Policy Communication

The risk management policy must: - Be communicated to all employees - Be available to stakeholders - Be regularly reviewed and updated - Be integrated into organizational documents

Risk Management Objectives

Strategic Objectives

  1. Value Creation
  2. Contribution to objective achievement
  3. Exploit opportunities

  4. Create competitive advantages

  5. Protection

  6. Protect assets
  7. Preserve reputation

  8. Ensure compliance

  9. Decision Quality

  10. Informed decisions
  11. Create transparency
  12. Manage uncertainties

Operational Objectives

  • Identify risks early
  • Assess risks appropriately
  • Implement effective measures
  • Continuously monitor and improve

Risk Appetite

Risk Appetite Definition

The organization defines: - Acceptable risk levels - Risk tolerances for different areas - Criteria for risk acceptance - Escalation thresholds

Risk Appetite Categories

Category Description Example
Strategic Risks to business objectives Market risks
Operational Risks in daily operations Process risks
Financial Financial risks Liquidity risks
Compliance Regulatory risks Legal changes

Resource Provision

Personnel Resources

  • Risk managers and team
  • Subject matter experts for risk analysis
  • Training and development
  • External consultants as needed

Financial Resources

  • Budget for risk management activities
  • Funds for risk mitigation measures
  • Investments in risk management systems
  • Insurance and provisions

Technical Resources

  • Risk management software
  • Data analysis tools
  • Communication platforms
  • Documentation systems

Leadership Activities

Regular Activities

  1. Risk Reviews
  2. Quarterly risk assessments
  3. Discussion of critical risks

  4. Decisions on measures

  5. Strategic Planning

  6. Include risks in strategy development
  7. Consider risk scenarios

  8. Make risk-based decisions

  9. Communication

  10. Promote risk awareness
  11. Communicate successes
  12. Create transparency

Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
CEO/Managing Director Overall responsibility for risk management
Board Oversight and strategic direction
CFO Financial risks and resources
Department Heads Risk management in their areas
Risk Manager Coordination and support

Success Measurement

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Number of identified risks
  • Percentage of treated risks
  • Effectiveness of measures
  • Risk management maturity
  • Employee engagement
