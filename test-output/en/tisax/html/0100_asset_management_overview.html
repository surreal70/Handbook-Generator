Scope

Asset Management Fundamentals

Definition of Assets

An asset is any resource of value to the organization, including:

  • Information Assets: Data, documents, databases
  • Software Assets: Applications, systems, development tools
  • Hardware Assets: Servers, workstations, mobile devices
  • Services: IT services, cloud services
  • Physical Assets: Facilities, infrastructure
  • Personnel Assets: Employees, service providers

Asset Management Objectives

  1. Complete overview of all assets
  2. Clear responsibilities for assets
  3. Appropriate protection based on value and criticality
  4. Traceability throughout the entire lifecycle

Asset Management Process

1. Asset Identification

[TODO] identifies assets through:

  • Systematic inventory
  • Continuous capture of new assets
  • Integration with IT asset management systems
  • Consideration of shadow IT

2. Asset Classification

Assets are classified according to:

  • Confidentiality: Public, Internal, Confidential, Strictly Confidential
  • Integrity: Low, Medium, High, Very High
  • Availability: Low, Medium, High, Critical
  • Business Value: Low, Medium, High, Critical

3. Asset Ownership

For each asset, the following is defined:

  • Asset Owner: Responsible for classification and protection measures
  • Asset Custodian: Responsible for technical administration
  • Asset User: Authorized to use

4. Asset Lifecycle

The asset lifecycle includes:

  1. Acquisition/Creation: Capture and classification
  2. Operation: Use and maintenance
  3. Change: Update of asset information
  4. Decommissioning: Secure disposal or archiving

Asset owners are responsible for:

Asset Inventory

Inventory Requirements

The asset inventory must contain:

  • Unique asset ID
  • Asset name and description
  • Asset type and category
  • Classification (confidentiality, integrity, availability)
  • Asset owner and custodian
  • Location (physical or logical)
  • Dependencies on other assets
  • License information (if applicable)

Inventory Maintenance

The asset inventory is maintained through:

  • Automated discovery tools
  • Manual capture
  • Regular audits (at least annually)
  • Change management integration

Protection Measures

Protection Based on Classification

Protection measures are implemented based on:

  • Asset classification
  • Threat analysis
  • Organization's risk acceptance
  • Regulatory requirements

Technical Protection Measures

  • Access control and authentication
  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Backup and recovery
  • Malware protection
  • Network segmentation

Organizational Protection Measures

  • Policies and procedures
  • Training and awareness
  • Contracts and agreements
  • Physical security measures
