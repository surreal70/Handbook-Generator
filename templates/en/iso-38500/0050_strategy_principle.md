## Purpose

This document describes the application of the Strategy principle in the organization's IT governance.

## Scope

This document applies to:
- {{ meta-organisation.name }}
- IT strategy and business strategy
- Strategic planning and alignment

## Principle Definition

The organization's IT strategy takes into account the current and future capabilities of IT and the strategic needs of the business.

## Evaluate

### Assess Strategic Alignment

- Does IT strategy support business strategy?
- Are IT capabilities aligned with business requirements?
- Are future technology trends considered?
- Is IT strategy aligned with stakeholders?

### Assessment Criteria

| Criterion | Target | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Strategic alignment | >90% | {{ meta-handbook.strategic_alignment }}% |
| Stakeholder satisfaction | >85% | {{ meta-handbook.stakeholder_satisfaction }}% |
| Technology currency | Current | {{ meta-handbook.technology_currency }} |

## Direct

### IT Strategy Components

1. **Vision and Mission**: IT vision for next 3-5 years
2. **Strategic Goals**: Measurable IT objectives
3. **Initiatives**: Strategic IT projects and programs
4. **Resource Planning**: Budgets, personnel, infrastructure
5. **Roadmap**: Timeline for strategic implementation

- Annual strategy planning
- Quarterly strategy reviews
- Adjustments as needed
- Communication to all stakeholders
