## Purpose

This document describes the risk management framework according to ISO 31000 Clause 5. It defines the structure and components for integrating risk management into the organization.

## Scope

This document applies to:
- {{ meta-organisation.name }}
- All organizational levels and functions
- Board, management, and risk management
- Risk management framework implementation

## Framework Overview

The risk management framework provides the foundation and organizational arrangements for designing, implementing, monitoring, reviewing, and continually improving risk management throughout the organization.

### Framework Purpose

The framework serves to:
- Integrate risk management into all organizational activities
- Ensure a consistent approach to risks
- Ensure the effectiveness of risk management
- Enable continual improvement

## Framework Components

### 1. Leadership and Commitment (Clause 5.3)

Top management must:
- Take responsibility for risk management
- Establish risk management policy and objectives
- Provide resources
- Integrate risk management into organizational processes

### 2. Integration (Clause 5.4)

Risk management must be integrated into:
- Organizational structure
- Leadership and management processes
- Decision-making
- Business processes and projects

### 3. Design (Clause 5.5)

Framework design considers:
- External and internal context
- Risk management commitment
- Accountabilities and authorities
- Resources
- Communication and reporting

### 4. Implementation (Clause 5.6)

Implementation includes:
- Develop implementation plan
- Define timeframes and responsibilities
- Apply risk management process
- Training and awareness building

### 5. Evaluation (Clause 5.7)

Regular evaluation of:
- Framework effectiveness
- Objective achievement
- Process suitability
- Organizational context changes

### 6. Improvement (Clause 5.8)

Continual improvement through:
- Adaptation to changes
- Learning from experiences
- Process optimization
- Promoting risk management culture

## Success Factors

1. **Top Management Support**: Visible leadership and commitment
2. **Clear Responsibilities**: Defined roles and authorities
3. **Adequate Resources**: Personnel, budget, systems
4. **Effective Communication**: Transparency and dialogue
5. **Continual Improvement**: Learning and adaptation
