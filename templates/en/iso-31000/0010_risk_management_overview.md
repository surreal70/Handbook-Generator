# Risk Management - Overview

## Purpose

This document describes the risk management framework according to ISO 31000. It defines the structure, principles, and processes for effective risk management in the organization.

## Scope

This document applies to:
- {{ meta.organization }}
- All business areas and processes
- Board, management, and risk management
- Risk management structures and processes

## Risk Management according to ISO 31000

ISO 31000 defines risk management as coordinated activities to direct and control an organization with regard to risk. Risk management includes the application of principles, a framework, and a process to identify, analyze, evaluate, and treat risks.

### Risk Management Objectives

1. **Value Creation**: Risk management contributes to achieving objectives
2. **Protection**: Risk management protects assets and reputation
3. **Decision Support**: Risk management improves decision-making
4. **Compliance**: Risk management meets legal requirements
5. **Continual Improvement**: Risk management promotes organizational development

## Risk Management Components

### Three Main Components

ISO 31000 is based on three integrated components:

- **Principles**: Eight fundamentals for effective risk management
- **Framework**: Structure for integration into the organization
- **Process**: Systematic approach to risk treatment

## Eight Risk Management Principles

1. **Integrated**: Risk management is part of all organizational activities
2. **Structured and comprehensive**: Systematic approach for consistent results
3. **Customized**: Risk management is aligned with context and objectives
4. **Inclusive**: Involvement of stakeholders for relevant insights
5. **Dynamic**: Risk management responds to changes
6. **Best available information**: Decisions based on current data
7. **Human and cultural factors**: Consideration of human behavior
8. **Continual improvement**: Ongoing development of risk management

## Risk Management Framework

### Framework Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Leadership and commitment | Top management support |
| Integration | Embedding in organizational processes |
| Design | Adaptation to organizational context |
| Implementation | Framework deployment |
| Evaluation | Effectiveness review |
| Improvement | Continual adaptation |

## Risk Management Process

1. **Communication and consultation**: Stakeholder engagement
2. **Establishing the context**: Define framework conditions
3. **Risk assessment**: Identification, analysis, evaluation
4. **Risk treatment**: Risk mitigation measures
5. **Monitoring and review**: Continuous control
6. **Recording and reporting**: Documentation and communication
