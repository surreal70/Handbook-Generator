# GDPR Data Protection Templates

## Overview

This template set provides comprehensive documentation templates for implementing and maintaining a Data Protection Management System in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR - EU 2016/679). The templates cover all relevant articles of the GDPR and provide a structured approach to documenting data protection processes, procedures, and evidence.

## GDPR Structure

The GDPR is organized into 11 chapters and 99 articles:

- **Chapter I (Art. 1-4):** General Provisions
- **Chapter II (Art. 5-11):** Principles
- **Chapter III (Art. 12-23):** Rights of the Data Subject
- **Chapter IV (Art. 24-43):** Controller and Processor
- **Chapter V (Art. 44-50):** Transfers of Personal Data to Third Countries
- **Chapter VI (Art. 51-59):** Independent Supervisory Authorities
- **Chapter VII (Art. 60-76):** Cooperation and Consistency
- **Chapter VIII (Art. 77-84):** Remedies, Liability and Penalties
- **Chapter IX (Art. 85-91):** Provisions Relating to Specific Processing Situations
- **Chapter X (Art. 92-93):** Delegated Acts and Implementing Acts
- **Chapter XI (Art. 94-99):** Final Provisions

## Usage Guidelines

### Customization

1. **Replace Placeholders:** All templates contain placeholders in the format `{{ source.field }}` or `[TODO]` that should be replaced with organization-specific information.

2. **Adapt to Context:** Modify templates to reflect your organization's size, complexity, and industry sector.

3. **Risk-Based Approach:** Consider the nature, scope, context and purposes of processing as well as the varying likelihood and severity of risks.

4. **National Adaptations:** Consider national data protection laws that supplement the GDPR.

### Document Control

- All templates include header fields for document control (ID, Owner, Version, Status, Classification, Last Update)
- Follow your organization's document control procedures when implementing these templates
- Maintain version history and change records

### Integration with Existing Systems

- Templates can be integrated with existing data protection management software
- Placeholder syntax supports automated data substitution from various sources
- HTML comments provide guidance for template authors
