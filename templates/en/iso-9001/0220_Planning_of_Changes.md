## Purpose

This document defines the process for planning changes to the quality management system in accordance with ISO 9001:2015 Clause 6.3.

## Change Management Process

### Types of Changes

**QMS changes may include:**
- Process changes
- Organizational structure changes
- Technology or system changes
- Product or service changes
- Resource changes
- Documentation changes
- Scope changes

### Change Initiation

**Changes may be initiated by:**
- Management review decisions
- Audit findings
- Corrective actions
- Improvement initiatives
- Risk assessments
- Customer requirements
- Regulatory changes
- Strategic decisions

## Change Planning Requirements

### Purpose of Change (6.3a)

**Document:**
- Why the change is needed
- What problem it solves or opportunity it addresses
- Expected benefits
- Alignment with quality objectives and strategy

### Potential Consequences (6.3b)

**Assess:**
- Impact on QMS effectiveness
- Impact on product/service conformity
- Impact on customer satisfaction
- Impact on compliance
- Risks introduced by the change
- Resource implications

### QMS Integrity (6.3c)

**Ensure:**
- QMS remains effective during and after change
- No gaps in QMS coverage
- Continued conformity to ISO 9001
- Maintained process interactions
- Continued achievement of intended results

### Resource Availability (6.3d)

**Identify:**
- Human resources needed
- Financial resources required
- Infrastructure and equipment needs
- Time requirements
- Training needs
- External support requirements

### Responsibilities and Authorities (6.3e)

**Define:**
- Who is responsible for change implementation
- Who has authority to approve change
- Who needs to be consulted
- Who needs to be informed
- Changes to organizational roles if applicable

## Change Implementation Planning

### Implementation Plan

**Plan includes:**
- Detailed implementation steps
- Timeline and milestones
- Resource allocation
- Responsibilities assignment
- Communication plan
- Training requirements
- Verification and validation activities
- Rollback plan if needed

### Risk Management

**Address:**
- Risks introduced by change
- Mitigation measures
- Contingency plans
- Monitoring during implementation

### Communication

**Communicate to:**
- Affected personnel
- Customers (if applicable)
- Suppliers (if applicable)
- Other interested parties

**Communication includes:**
- What is changing
- Why it is changing
- When it will change
- How it affects them
- What actions they need to take

## Implementation and Verification

### Implementation

- Execute according to plan
- Monitor progress
- Address issues promptly
- Document implementation

### Verification

**Verify:**
- Change implemented as planned
- Intended results achieved
- No unintended consequences
- QMS integrity maintained
- Resources adequate

### Validation

**Validate:**
- Change effectiveness
- Objectives met
- Customer requirements still met
- Compliance maintained
