## COSO Framework

### Overview

The COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework is a globally recognized framework for designing, implementing, and conducting internal control, and for assessing its effectiveness.

**Publisher**: Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission (COSO)

**Current Version**: COSO 2013 Framework (updated)

### Framework Structure

The COSO Framework consists of:

1. **Five Components** of internal control
2. **17 Principles** that support the components
3. **Points of Focus** for each principle

### The Five Components

#### 1. Control Environment

The control environment provides the foundation for all other components of internal control.

**Principles**:
- Principle 1: Integrity and ethical values
- Principle 2: Board independence
- Principle 3: Organizational structure and responsibilities
- Principle 4: Competence
- Principle 5: Accountability

#### 2. Risk Assessment

The organization identifies and analyzes risks to the achievement of its objectives.

**Principles**:
- Principle 6: Specification of objectives
- Principle 7: Risk identification
- Principle 8: Fraud assessment
- Principle 9: Change identification

#### 3. Control Activities

Actions established through policies and procedures that help ensure management directives are carried out.

**Principles**:
- Principle 10: Selection and development of control activities
- Principle 11: Technology controls
- Principle 12: Deployment through policies and procedures

#### 4. Information and Communication

The organization obtains or generates and uses relevant, quality information.

**Principles**:
- Principle 13: Information quality
- Principle 14: Internal communication
- Principle 15: External communication

#### 5. Monitoring Activities

Ongoing and separate evaluations enable determination of whether components of internal control are present and functioning.

**Principles**:
- Principle 16: Ongoing and separate evaluations
- Principle 17: Evaluation and communication of deficiencies

## Internal Control Objectives

The COSO Framework supports the organization in achieving objectives in three categories:

### 1. Operations Objectives

- Effectiveness and efficiency of operations
- Protection of assets
- Optimization of resource utilization

### 2. Reporting Objectives

- Reliability of financial reporting
- Accuracy of non-financial reporting
- Timely reporting

### 3. Compliance Objectives

- Compliance with laws and regulations
- Compliance with internal policies
- Fulfillment of contractual obligations

## Integration with Other Frameworks

### Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)

The COSO Framework is closely linked to the COSO ERM Framework and supports integrated risk management.

### SOC 1 / SOC 2

COSO provides the foundation for SOC reports and defines control criteria.

### Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)

COSO is the most commonly used framework for SOX 404 compliance.

### ISO 31000

Complementary application for risk management processes.

## Effectiveness Assessment

### Criteria for Effective Internal Control

Internal control is considered effective when:

1. All five components are present and functioning
2. All 17 principles are present and functioning
3. The components function together in an integrated manner
